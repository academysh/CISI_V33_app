Chapter 3, Section 3 — Detailed Deep Dive
Market Manipulation · Syllabus 3.3 · CISI V33
What is this section about?
The second "half" of market abuse (after insider dealing) is market manipulation. UK MAR states that a person shall not engage in or attempt to engage in market manipulation. There are 4 forms: (1) manipulating transactions, (2) manipulating devices, (3) dissemination, (4) benchmark manipulation. The section also covers legitimate behaviour, accepted market practices (AMPs), the obligations of PDMRs, and the rules on investment recommendations. 2-3 exam questions, often scenarios asking which form of manipulation applies.

3.1

General Prohibition + 4 forms of manipulation

UK MAR states that a person shall not engage or attempt to engage in market manipulation. Market manipulation can cause distortion of financial markets, users may get a misleading impression, and it undermines investor trust.

Attempts also prohibited
UK MAR explicitly prohibits both engaging in AND attempting to engage in manipulation. Even unsuccessful attempts constitute a violation.

▸4 forms of market manipulation

1
Manipulating Transactions — behaviour consisting of effecting transactions / orders not for legitimate reasons + not in conformity with accepted practices, which give a false or misleading impression of supply/demand/price of the qualifying investment, OR secure the price at an abnormal/artificial level. Examples: "wash trades", "spoofing", pump-and-dump through real orders.
2
Manipulating Devices — effecting transactions / orders using fictitious devices or any other form of deception / contrivance. Examples: fake news + coordinated trading, using multiple accounts to imitate genuine activity.
3
Dissemination — dissemination of information by any means, giving a false or misleading impression of a qualifying investment, by a person who knew, or could reasonably be expected to have known, the info was false / misleading. This is "particularly harmful" — investors make decisions on the basis of incorrect/distorted information.
4
Benchmark Manipulation — transmitting false/misleading inputs in relation to a benchmark, where the person knew or ought to have known the input was false/misleading, OR any other behaviour manipulating the calculation of a benchmark. Classic: LIBOR / FX fix scandals of the 2010s.
Scenario — identify the form
• Trader buys shares across multiple personal accounts, creating a false impression of demand → Manipulating transactions
• Algo bot posts layered orders cancelled before execution → Manipulating devices (spoofing)
• Journalist publishes a fake story about a takeover → Dissemination
• LIBOR submitter submits a rate benefiting own bank's trading position → Benchmark manipulation
3.2

Legitimate Behaviour + AMPs + PDMRs

▸Legitimate Behaviour

Legitimate behaviour in relation to market manipulation — actions/transactions carried out for valid commercial reasons, in line with accepted market practices.

▸Accepted Market Practices (AMPs)

AMPs — FCA criteria
The FCA may accept practices as legitimate. Criteria for establishing an AMP:
  • Transparency of the practice
  • Effect on market liquidity / efficiency
  • Interaction with the market mechanism
  • Risk to integrity of directly/indirectly-related markets
  • Outcome of the FCA's investigation of past practice
  • Structural characteristics of the relevant market

▸PDMRs — Persons Discharging Managerial Responsibilities

PDMR — definition
PDMRs are members of the issuer's administrative, management or supervisory bodies (e.g. directors) + senior executives with regular access to inside information + power to make managerial decisions affecting future developments.

▸PDMR obligations

  • Notify issuer + FCA of transactions in the issuer's instruments, within 3 business days
  • Applies to transactions ≥ €5,000 per year (aggregate)
  • Same rules apply to closely associated persons (spouse, dependent children, controlled entities)
  • Closed periods — NO trading in the issuer's instruments during the 30 calendar days BEFORE announcement of the interim / annual financial report
Closed period exceptions
Trading during the closed period may be permitted in exceptional circumstances:
  • Severe financial need requiring immediate realisation of funds
  • Employee share schemes with no change in beneficial ownership
Never "whenever approved by the FCA". There is no €10k threshold.
3.3

Investment Recommendations

Investment recommendations — research notes, reports, recommendations produced by firms for clients or general distribution. UK MAR sets specific obligations so that recommendations are fair, objective, clearly presented.

▸Key requirements

  • Identity of the producer must be clear (individual + firm)
  • All substantially material sources — clearly/prominently indicated
  • Facts clearly distinguished from interpretations, estimates, opinions, and other non-factual information
  • Projections, valuations, price targets — clearly labelled as such, with material assumptions
  • Previous recommendations (12 months) — if the new one differs from a previous one, indicate the change + the date of the previous recommendation
  • Any conflicts of interest — disclosed (firm holds a position, proprietary trading, received compensation from the issuer)
Conflict disclosure example
A firm holds a large long position in an instrument and publishes a "buy" recommendation. That is a conflict. The firm must disclose it — readers should understand the recommendation may be biased by the firm's own exposure.
📋 Final summary of Section 3
4 forms of market manipulation
(1) Manipulating transactions — fake orders/trades, not legitimate
(2) Manipulating devices — fictitious devices, deception
(3) Dissemination — spreading false info (especially harmful)
(4) Benchmark manipulation — false inputs (LIBOR-type)
  • 3.1 General prohibition: UK MAR prohibits engaging AND attempting to engage in market manipulation.
  • 4 forms:
    • (1) Manipulating transactions — not legitimate + false impression of supply/demand/price OR price at abnormal level
    • (2) Manipulating devices — fictitious devices, deception, contrivance
    • (3) Dissemination — spreading false info (knew or should have known) — "particularly harmful"
    • (4) Benchmark manipulation — false inputs to a benchmark
  • 3.2 Legitimate behaviour: valid commercial reasons + accepted market practices. FCA criteria for AMPs — transparency, effect on liquidity, risk to market integrity.
  • PDMRs:
    • Directors + senior executives with regular access to inside info
    • Notify issuer + FCA within 3 business days
    • Threshold: ≥ €5,000 aggregate transactions per year
    • Rules apply to closely associated persons
    • Closed period: 30 calendar days BEFORE financial reports
    • Exceptions: severe financial need + employee share schemes with no change in beneficial ownership
  • 3.3 Investment recommendations:
    • Identity of producer clear
    • Material sources — clearly indicated
    • Facts distinguished from opinions
    • Projections — labelled with material assumptions
    • Previous recommendations (12 months) — if differs, indicate change + date
    • Conflicts of interest — disclosed
