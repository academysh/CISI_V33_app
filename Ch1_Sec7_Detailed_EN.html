Chapter 1, Section 7 — Detailed Deep Dive
Training and Competence · Syllabus 1.8 · CISI V33
What is this section about?
Section 7 is about the competence of people working in financial services. The logic: the FCA requires firms to make sure every employee involved in a regulated activity has sufficient knowledge and skills. This is anchored in Principle 3 (management and control) + the T&C Sourcebook. Key point: an employee cannot carry out a regulated activity unsupervised until the firm has assessed them as competent. Plus CPD (Continuing Professional Development) — 35 hours/year for retail investment advisers + SPS (Statement of Professional Standing) from accredited bodies (CISI). This section interlocks with the SM&CR Certification Regime. On the exam 1-2 questions.

7.1

Overall Requirements — Principle 3 + SYSC

Principle for Businesses 3 (management and control) requires firms to take reasonable care to organise and control their affairs responsibly and effectively. To comply, firms must ensure that any employee involved with a regulated activity achieves and maintains the competence needed for their role.

▸Where the rules live

The Principles are built on further in SYSC (Senior Management Arrangements, Systems and Controls) — in the FCA Handbook + PRA Rule Book. SYSC incorporates a high-level competence requirement that applies to ALL UK-authorised firms — including wholesale firms.

High-level (SYSC) vs detailed (T&C Sourcebook)
SYSC — high-level, applies to all firms (retail and wholesale).
T&C Sourcebook — detailed, applies to firms carrying on activities with retail customers (plus some limited cases for consumers/customers).

The T&C Sourcebook is more focused on the outcomes achieved by firms through their internal training arrangements.

▸Scope of the T&C Sourcebook

The T&C Sourcebook supplements SYSC for firms carrying on activities with or for retail customers only and in some limited cases for customers and consumers.

Practical coverage
Wholesale trading desk (institutional clients) → only SYSC high-level competence.
IFA advising retail clients → SYSC + T&C Sourcebook (detailed rules on qualifications, CPD, supervision).
7.2

Requirements for Retail Firms: Assessment + Supervision

▸Assessment rule — competence

The assessment rule
The T&C Sourcebook requires firms not to deem an employee competent to carry on specified activities until:
  • The employee has demonstrated the necessary competence
  • Has passed each module of an appropriate examination (if required)
The assessment does not have to take place before they start to carry on the activity — but without the assessment they must be supervised.

▸Supervision until assessed competent

Firms must not allow an employee to carry on any specified activities without appropriate supervision. They are required to ensure employees are appropriately supervised AT ALL TIMES.

Supervision is more intense before competence
The FCA expects the level and intensity of supervision to be significantly greater in the period BEFORE a firm has assessed an employee as competent, than after. Firms should have clear criteria and procedures relating to the specific point at which their employees are assessed as competent — to demonstrate when and why a reduced level of supervision was considered appropriate.

At all stages, firms are required to consider the level of relevant experience an employee has — when determining the level of supervision required.

▸Requirements for supervisors

There are additional requirements in respect of those supervising staff carrying out specified activities:

  • Firms must ensure supervisors have the necessary coaching and assessment skills
  • Plus the technical knowledge associated with the activity — to act as a competent supervisor and assessor
  • The Sourcebook says firms should consider whether it is appropriate to require supervisors to pass an appropriate examination, if the staff they supervise have not yet been assessed as competent
Packaged products — the supervisor must pass an examination
If the employee is advising on packaged products to retail customers — the firm must ensure the supervisor has passed an appropriate examination. This is stricter than the general rule.
7.3

Qualification Requirements BEFORE Starting Activities

The T&C Sourcebook says firms must not allow employees to carry on/undertake a specified activity (other than an overseeing activity) for which there is a qualification requirement, WITHOUT first having passed the relevant regulatory module of an appropriate qualification.

▸Activities requiring a qualification before starting

Firms must not allow employees to undertake ANY of the following activities unless they have passed each module of an appropriate qualification:

  • Certain advising and dealing activities
  • Acting as a broker fund adviser
  • Advising on syndicate participation at Lloyd's
  • Acting as a pension transfer specialist
The Appropriate Qualifications Table
The FCA publishes qualifications tables (TC App 4), containing the appropriate qualifications that need to be attained by employees — to carry out certain activities for retail clients. The activities are set out in Appendix 1.1.1R of the T&C Sourcebook.

▸Activities to which qualifications apply

The qualification requirements apply to individuals carrying out one or more of the following regulated activities:

  • Advising on (but not dealing in) securities (which are not stakeholder pension schemes or broker funds)
  • Advising on (but not dealing in) derivatives
  • Advising on packaged products (which are not broker funds)
  • Advising on friendly society tax-exempt policies
  • Advising on and dealing in securities (not stakeholder pension schemes or broker funds)
  • Advising on and dealing with or for clients in derivatives

▸Older qualifications — gap-filling

If a qualification is no longer on the current Table
If an individual holds an older qualification that is no longer on the FCA's current Appropriate Qualifications Table, the firm must assess whether additional gap-filling training is needed — to bring the individual up to current standards. Old qualifications may not cover all current regulatory requirements. The firm takes responsibility for ensuring competence.

▸Overseas experience exemption

Overseas experience — 3 year minimum
For a qualification exemption based on relevant overseas experience, a minimum of 3 years' relevant overseas experience is required. The firm must carefully assess whether the experience maps to UK regulatory requirements.
7.4

Statements of Professional Standing (SPS)

Under the Adviser Charging rules, individuals wishing to operate as a retail investment adviser must be in possession of a valid SPS from an FCA-accredited body (e.g. CISI).

▸What an SPS confirms

An SPS confirms that the adviser has:

  • Completed a Qualification and Credit Framework (QCF) Level 4 qualification appearing on the list of approved qualifications in the FCA Handbook
  • Completed an annual programme of 35 hours of CPD meeting FCA requirements
  • Acted in accordance with the FCA's APER and/or SM&CR requirements
  • Agreed to adhere to a code of ethics

▸SPS validity + contents

SPS details
An SPS is valid for 12 months. It contains:
  • The adviser's name
  • The name and contact details of the accredited body + a named signatory
  • The end date of verification (max 12 months from the verification date)
  • Confirmation that the adviser's qualifications have been verified
  • Confirmation that the adviser has signed an annual declaration:
    • They have kept their knowledge up to date
    • They follow the accredited body's code of ethics

▸Accredited bodies

Accredited bodies (such as CISI) are approved by the FCA to issue SPSs. They have their own accreditation standards + code of ethics.

7.4.1

CPD — Continuing Professional Development

▸35 hours per year

FCA minimum CPD requirement
The FCA has said that advisers must complete a minimum of 35 hours of relevant CPD each year, with at least 21 hours being structured learning.
CPD breakdownHours
Total CPD per year35 hours
Structured learning (minimum)21 hours
Unstructured learning (max)14 hours

▸Structured vs unstructured CPD

Structured — formal courses, seminars, webinars, workshops, exams with learning outcomes.
Unstructured — reading professional publications, self-study, informal discussions, research.

▸Professional bodies — higher standards

FCA = floor; professional bodies may set higher
The FCA sets the minimum CPD standard. Professional bodies (CISI) may set higher CPD requirements. They cannot go below the FCA minimum, but can exceed it.

CISI, for example, requires members to meet CISI-specific CPD requirements — which may be stricter than the FCA's.
7.5

Link between T&C and the SM&CR Certification Regime

T&C rules + the SM&CR Certification Regime work together. Under the Certification Regime, firms must assess fitness and propriety annually. CPD is a key mechanism for demonstrating that certified persons maintain ongoing competence.

CPD + the Certification Regime
Firms must annually attest that individuals captured by the Certification Regime remain fit and proper. CPD records are a significant piece of evidence for this attestation.

▸Certification vs SMF — both need CPD

CPD applies to both:

  • Senior Managers (SMFs) — approved by the regulator
  • Certified persons — certified by the firm annually

The Certification Regime did not abolish CPD — it reinforces it.

▸Scenario — passing the exam is not enough

Exam scenario — Tom's case
Tom has just joined a wealth management firm. He has passed the relevant CISI examination, but the firm has not yet formally assessed his competence.

Can Tom provide investment advice to retail clients?

NO — passing the exam is necessary but not sufficient. The firm must formally assess competence before Tom can carry on the activity unsupervised. Until then — work under the supervision of a competent person.
