Record-Keeping Requirements · Syllabus 3.5 · CISI V33
What is this section about?
Record-keeping — a tangential but important aspect of AML and financial crime prevention. Keeping records of evidence of identity, transactions, and training allows the FCA and authorities to reconstruct what happened during an investigation. The baseline rule: 5 years retention for all AML records. MLR 2017, SYSC 9, and MiFID II all require 5-year retention. 1-2 exam questions — often about specific periods (5 vs 3 vs 7 years).

5.1

AML/CFT Record-Keeping Requirements

Under MLR 2017, firms must retain records that demonstrate compliance with AML/CFT (Combating the Financing of Terrorism) obligations.

▸Specified periods — all 5 years

RecordRetentionStart from
Evidence of identity5 yearsEnd of relationship with the client
Transactions5 yearsDate the transaction was completed
Other records5 yearsObtaining the information / creation of the record

▸AML training records

Training records must include
The records kept must include:
  • Dates when AML training was given
  • Nature of the training
  • Names of staff who received the training

▸Reliance on third parties

Where a firm relies on another party to carry out AML checks (e.g. a third-party KYC provider), the firm must demonstrate how it satisfies itself that the other party's records are being retained for 5 years.

Conversely, where a firm is relied upon by others for CDD checks, it must retain records for the same 5-year period applying to its own CDD.

FCA Handbook — SYSC 6 + SYSC 9
The FCA's general record-keeping requirements are in SYSC 6 and SYSC 9. Firms must take reasonable care to make and retain adequate records.

▸Ongoing monitoring

Part of CDD — ongoing monitoring of existing relationships:

  • Validate that customer information remains current
  • Detect unusual transaction patterns potentially indicating ML/TF
  • Update identification records + customer risk profile
Exam trap — 3 vs 5 years
Be careful on the exam: non-MiFID complaints = 3 years; MiFID complaints = 5 years. AML records — always 5 years. Not 3. Not 7. Not 10.
📋 Final summary of Section 5
Golden rule — 5 YEARS
AML evidence of identity = 5y (from end of relationship)
AML transactions = 5y (from date completed)
MiFID transactions = 5y
MiFID complaints = 5y
Suitability = 5y (min)
  • Base MLR 2017 rule: 3 categories of records — all 5 years:
    • Evidence of identity — 5y from end of client relationship
    • Transactions — 5y from date transaction completed
    • Other — 5y from obtaining information / creation of record
  • AML training records must include: dates + nature of training + names of staff who received it.
  • Third-party reliance: the firm must demonstrate how it satisfies itself that the other party's records are retained for 5 years.
  • Other 5-year records: MiFID transaction records, client orders, STOR supporting docs, suitability reports, MiFID complaints.
  • Exception: non-MiFID complaints = 3 years (not 5).
  • FCA source: SYSC 6 + SYSC 9 — general record-keeping requirements.
  • Ongoing monitoring — part of CDD — validate customer info + detect unusual patterns.
